Reclaiming Rainfed Resilience

Siri Dhanya (Millet) Cultivation as a Water-Sustainable and Nutritionally Superior Alternative to Green Revolution Monocultures in Telangana, India

Rayilla Sai Prakash · M.Sc. Geoinformatics · Study area: Jagtial–Karimnagar region, Telangana

Download Full Research Paper

Project Overview

This interdisciplinary study examines whether locally cultivated minor millets can provide a more water-sustainable and nutritionally valuable alternative to irrigation-intensive cropping systems in semi-arid Telangana. It combines participant-observation fieldwork and community agronomic knowledge with secondary water-productivity evidence and a proposed GIS framework for identifying districts suitable for millet conversion.

Research Aims

  • Document locally reported water requirements, crop calendars and agronomic practices for millet cultivation.
  • Compare millet nutrient composition with rice and wheat using the cited Telugu-language Siridhanya dataset.
  • Situate field observations within Telangana and Karimnagar water-productivity evidence.
  • Develop a replicable GIS methodology for identifying water-stressed districts where millet conversion may offer the greatest sustainability benefit.

Methods and Data

Participant Observation

Field engagement with farming communities in the Jagtial–Karimnagar region documented cultivation practices, crop calendars, water-use estimates, yields and mixed-cropping knowledge.

Secondary Analysis

State- and district-level water-productivity evidence was synthesised from the ICRIER–NABARD assessment of major Indian crops.

Nutrient Dataset

Nutrient values were drawn from the cited Telugu-language Siridhanya source and are presented as source-reported values rather than independently re-analysed laboratory results.

Atavi Krushi: Forest-Style Polyculture

The research documents a mixed-crop model combining millet strips with groundnut, sesame, gongura, legumes and oilseeds. Rainwater-harvesting trenches capture surface runoff, while moringa, custard apple and guava planted on trench berms add an agroforestry component. The model is framed as an ecological approach oriented toward long-term soil and water stewardship.

Spatial and GIS Research Direction

The present paper provides a spatial-analytical synthesis rather than a completed original GIS analysis. Its proposed next phase is a QGIS-based district suitability model combining:

  • Central Ground Water Board groundwater-stress classifications;
  • district-level rice and sugarcane cropped-area data;
  • India Meteorological Department rainfall normals; and
  • a weighted millet-conversion suitability index for Telangana districts.

Key Conclusions and Recommendations

  • Conduct controlled field trials with agricultural universities or ICAR-IIMR to verify community-reported water-use and yield estimates.
  • Strengthen procurement and price support for minor millets rather than relying on agronomic extension alone.
  • Execute the proposed GIS suitability analysis to prioritise water-stressed districts.
  • Support trench-based rainwater harvesting, mixed cropping and agroforestry during transitions from paddy to millet systems.

Research Status

Ongoing independent research. The field documentation and analytical paper are complete in their present form; experimental validation and the proposed district-level QGIS suitability analysis remain future phases.
