About Me


I am Rayilla Sai Prakash, an early-career researcher from Hyderabad, India, with an interdisciplinary academic background in Linguistics and Geo-Informatics.


My primary research interests include sociolinguistics, language variation and change, multilingualism, language contact, heritage language maintenance, dialectology, and language documentation. I am particularly interested in documenting the linguistic diversity of Telangana and understanding how languages evolve across communities.


Alongside linguistics, I have experience in Geo-Informatics, spatial analysis, GIS, and environmental research. My interdisciplinary interests allow me to integrate language documentation with digital technologies and geospatial methods.
