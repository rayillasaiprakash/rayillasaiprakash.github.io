Research

My research combines sociolinguistics, language variation, multilingualism, language documentation, and Geo-Informatics. I am particularly interested in documenting the linguistic diversity of Telangana while also exploring interdisciplinary applications of spatial technologies in environmental and sustainability research.

Linguistics Research

Karimnagar Dialect

Master's thesis exploring regional linguistic variation in Telangana Telugu through dialect documentation and linguistic analysis.

Language Variation & Change

Research on variation in Telugu across regions, speakers, and social contexts.

Language Documentation

Field-based documentation of Telangana dialects, vocabulary, oral traditions, and linguistic diversity.

Migration & Multilingualism

Future research on multilingual communities, language contact, and heritage language maintenance.

Geo-Informatics Research

Urbanisation Impact on Groundwater Resources

M.Sc. dissertation using GIS, Remote Sensing, and spatial analysis to examine groundwater changes associated with urban development.

Siri Dhanya (Millets) Sustainability Research

Independent research investigating sustainable millet cultivation using Geo-Informatics, environmental analysis, and climate resilience perspectives.

Current Research Focus

  • Regional linguistic variation in Telangana Telugu.
  • Language documentation and digital archiving.
  • Telangana Proverbs and Festivals documentation.
  • Migration, multilingualism, and heritage language maintenance.
  • Geo-Informatics applications in sustainability and agriculture.

Future Research Goals

  • Pursue a fully funded Ph.D. in Linguistics.
  • Publish research in sociolinguistics and language variation.
  • Develop the Telangana Language Documentation Project into a digital archive.
  • Expand interdisciplinary research combining Linguistics and Geo-Informatics.
