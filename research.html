Research

My research combines sociolinguistics, language variation, multilingualism, language documentation, and Geo-Informatics. I am particularly interested in documenting the linguistic diversity of Telangana while also exploring interdisciplinary applications of spatial technologies in environmental and sustainability research.

Completed Master’s Theses

M.A. Linguistics Thesis: Karimnagar Dialect

Exploring Regional Linguistic Variation: The Karimnagar Dialect in Focus
Completed M.A. thesis examining regional linguistic variation in Telangana Telugu through dialect documentation and linguistic analysis.

M.Sc. Geo-Informatics Thesis: Urbanisation Impact on Groundwater Resources

Completed M.Sc. dissertation using GIS, Remote Sensing, and spatial analysis to examine groundwater changes associated with urban development.

Current Research & Ongoing Projects

Language Variation & Change

Ongoing research interest in variation in Telugu across regions, speakers, and social contexts.

Telangana Language Documentation

Ongoing field-based documentation of Telangana dialects, vocabulary, oral traditions, and linguistic diversity.

Telangana Proverbs & Festivals Documentation

Current documentation and digital-archive work on proverbs, festivals, cultural knowledge, and regional language use.

Migration & Multilingualism

Research interest in multilingual communities, language contact, migration, and heritage-language maintenance.

Siri Dhanya (Millets) Sustainability Research

Ongoing independent research on sustainable millet cultivation, water productivity, nutrition, agroecology, and Geo-Informatics applications.

GIS Applications in Sustainability & Agriculture

Current interdisciplinary interest in applying GIS, Remote Sensing, and spatial analysis to environmental and agricultural research.
